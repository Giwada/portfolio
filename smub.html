Overview

This is a 128-day academic project to turn a place in the city centre called Hobujaama from a thoughtless place to a more meaningful place where people can spend great time and get empowered emotionally. The collaboration with Tallinn City Government, Kalasatama Urban Lab in Helsinki, and Distributed Design made the project possible.

Role


Strategic Design

I've taken initiative in contextual research, internal brainstorming, sketching/CAD urban modelling, and project documentation in collaboration with the interdisciplinary team.

RESEARCH


To understand and empathise with the users around the Hobujaama, define pain points, and opportunity areas, we conducted observation and interview there.

To interpret and analyse gathered data, we created 'Giga Map' which combined several kinds of maps e.g. user groups, stakeholders, experience journey, trends, and relations and connections.

IDEATION


Since the place lacks color and nature, we explored opportunity areas to fill the gap and came up with combining Bog (wetland) and technology for addressing human’s 5 senses.

After several brainstorming sessions, We reached one concept - “Smart Modular Urban Bog (SMUB)” which implements modularity and smartness into the urban environment to create more peacefull atmosphere.

PROTOTYPE & TEST


We started from creating a landscape plan so as to describe SMUB from bird’s view and end with a minimal modular allocation plan. After that, we defined how it works, what happens, what people could do there, how it changes people’s behaviour.

Users can get a relaxation effect through their five senses, for instance, people can get coolness and warmness around the natural air conditioning on the pine-tree benches. This relaxation effect is intended to change the meaning of Hobujaama from a voiceless to peaceful place.

CONCLUSION


With SMUB, people can not only get a relaxation through several sensations all together but also have an engagement on the place through an inspirational and educational environment.

And Hobujaama has a potential to become a leading model case which balances citizens, community, and environment.
